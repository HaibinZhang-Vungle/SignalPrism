Model tuning evidence

Model tuning has hit a plateau.

Round 1 is a self-contained conclusion: within the current 23-feature DCNv2 input set, architecture and HP changes no longer move validation loss in a meaningful way. The next lever is not another wide search over the same signal; it is new feature signal.

Evidence Readout

Round 1 observed facts: capacity, late training, and gradient behavior.
Capacity is not the blocker. Wide, small, cross=1, cross=3, cross=5, emb=16, and emb=32 land in the same validation band when training is healthy.
Late epochs barely move. T004 validation loss changes from 1.1565399e-5 to 1.1565354e-5 from epoch 16 to 20, about 0.0004% relative improvement.
Gradient is effectively gone. T004 gradient norm falls to 1.57e-8 by epoch 12 and then stays at zero in later epochs.

Round 1 Conclusion

This is the standalone decision from the HP sweep.
decision
evidence Different HP axes converge to the same band.

Network width, cross depth, embedding size, and learning-rate variants do not produce a durable validation-loss improvement once training is healthy.

interpretation The current feature set is the bottleneck.

The loss plateau plus near-zero gradients says the model has largely exhausted the learnable signal available in the current 23 features.

status HP-only tuning has limited remaining upside.

The remaining improvement is unlikely to come from another HP-only sweep because tested architecture and optimization axes already converge to the same validation-loss band.

Feature gap proposal

High-error dimensions define the feature search.

Round 2 starts from the Round 1 bottleneck and asks where the missing signal lives. Phase 1A localizes the top drill-down pocket. Phase 1B now focuses on that one segment: appopen, US, admob, android. The goal is to find wide-table fields that split this pocket into more actionable subgroups.

Old Pipeline vs New Feature Search

Round 2 proposes a cheaper screening loop before full model integration: use residual pockets, wide-table raw fields, and distribution evidence first.

Proposal: low-cost evidence before training. The model pipeline is used after candidates already look plausible.

Suggested narrative: Round 1 did not fail. It completed the optimization search for the current signal set. Round 2 then combines Phase 1A Stage2 CART and multidim drill-down with Phase 1B feature-gap checks to propose a scoped candidate set under high-error dimensions: price distribution, revenue/request history, appopen supply/OS context, and floor-positioning ratios. Phase 1C is left out until its interpretation is reliable; the next proof step is model-side ablation plus holdout or offline simulation.
